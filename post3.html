Female Trailblazers in the World of Data Science

A Word Cloud

A while ago, I was perusing through Google and I found a library called D3.js in which you can create various types of data visualizations.

What is a Word Cloud?

Word cloud is a process in which we statistically analyze which words have the highest frequency. The words with a higher frequency have a larger font size in comparison to the words that have a lower frequency. In this word cloud, Data Science has the highest frequency. In addition, stop words have been removed from this word cloud to remove any distractions from the final result.

What is this Word Cloud about?

I found an article on Analytics Vidhya celebrating the power of women in data science. Women around the world are blazing a trail in the data science world and what better way to honor some of these women than to make a word cloud about them. These powerful women are inspiring both women AND men in the world of data science. I also found an article on Galvanize listing some of the top female trailblazers working in the data science industry.

Who are the 10 women that make up this Word Cloud?

While caring for patients during the war, Florence Nightingale witnessed the atrocious conditions at the British military hospital in Turkey. The hospital was chaotic, the mortality rate was high, and records were poorly kept. She decided to use statistical analysis to determine best practices at the hospital, and to save lives. Nightingale’s charts weren’t just filled with numbers. It could be argued that she is the godmother of “infographics.” Her most famous design was the “coxcomb,” which is like a more intricate pie chart. The coxcomb is still used to this day.

Mary Lucy Cartwright is the grandmother of modern data science. Cartwright came from a wealthy family. She was educated at the finest schools, including Leamington High School in England, where a female mathematics teacher took a special interest in her. Cartwright then went on to study at St. Hugh’s College at Oxford University to study mathematics, but found herself pushed out of lectures, which she needed to graduate, by men returning from World War I. She half considered giving up and majoring in history instead, but she decided that math was the better path. She caught a lucky break at a party, where a professor suggested she attend some evening classes with a geometry professor, Godfrey H. Harding. That propelled her on her way to a degree in mathematics.

Katherine Johnson is one of the more famous names on our list of most influential women data scientists. She was featured in both a book and movie called Hidden Figures, about the women who worked behind the scenes but were critical parts of the 1960s space program.

Radhika Kulkarni While Radhika Kulkarni is technically retired, she remains one of the most influential women in data science. For 35 years, she worked in operations research, artificial intelligence, and machine learning. She was Vice President of Advanced Analytics R&D, overseeing software development in several analytical areas, including forecasting and data mining, operations research and econometrics. She serves on several boards and she received the Women in Operations Research and the Management Sciences Award. Since her recent retirement, Kulkarni has worked to increase data literacy and encourage more people to enter the field of data science.

Emily Glassberg Sands is a Ph.D in Economics from Harvard University. She has an impressive list of awards and honors she won while at Harvard. In her role as the Head of Data Science at Coursera, her mission is to build a better learning platform through data-drive decisions and products.

Carla Gentry is currently working as the Digital Marketing Manager at Samtec Inc. She has over 20 years worth of experience working for companies like Johnson & Johnson, Hershey, Kraft, among others. She is a very popular and active social media user and her posts are always worth reading for their invaluable knowledge.

Cassie Kozyrkov is the Chief Decision Scientist at Google. She is a well-known speaker in the data science sphere, and often pens down her thoughts in articulate fashion in this field. In her DataHack Radio podcast episode, she takes us on a journey into her life at Google and how she went from being a Statistician at Google to her current role.

Vivian Zhang is the CTO and Chief Data Scientist at the popular NYC Data Science Academy. She has almost a decade’s worth of experience in the field of research and data science. She was featured in the list of “9 Women Leading the Pack in Data Analytics” by Forbes in August 2016.

Dr. Elena Grewal is the head of Data Science at Airbnb. She is leading a team of 100+ data scientists. She did her graduation in economics and political science from Yale and spent a year in India working on a project to bring clean water to children who had acute diarrhea.

Caitlin Smallwood is the Vice President of Science and Analytics at Netflix. Her team develops new models and algorithms that directly improve the Netflix service. At Netflix, she was previously leading a team of mathematicians, statisticians and data scientists who worked on algorithms research, development, predictive modeling to bring to you a seamless experience while using Netflix. She is one of the leading figures in the data science community.
